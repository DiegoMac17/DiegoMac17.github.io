About Me

I'm a Ph.D. candidate in Biomedical Engineering at Rensselaer Polytechnic Institute, specializing in medical AI research. My work focuses on developing multimodal AI models for disease diagnosis, with particular emphasis on neurological conditions like Parkinson's and Alzheimer's disease.

With experience at IBM Research and as an NIA Alzheimer's Disease Clinical and Translational Research Training Program Fellow, I've developed expertise in multimodal data fusion, representation learning, and applying large language models to healthcare challenges. My research aims to create interpretable and generalizable AI solutions that can improve patient outcomes and advance precision medicine.

Beyond my research, I serve as Chief Scientific Officer at AduCare, a startup focused on Alzheimer's patient management, and as President of the CBIS Graduate and Postdoctoral Student Association, where I organize professional development events and foster community among researchers.

Developed a multi-modal foundation model for discovering associations between genotypes and brain imaging phenotypes using self-supervised learning and transformer architectures.

Language Modeling for Parkinson's Disease Screening

Created an AI model that leverages language models to screen for Parkinson's disease using patient self-reported questionnaires, addressing challenges of data sparsity and interpretability.

Subtyping Alzheimer’s Using Multimodal Transformer

Developed attention-based deep learning models for efficient genotype, brain imaging, and clinical-tabular data encoding towards patient classification and imaging-genomics feature association.
